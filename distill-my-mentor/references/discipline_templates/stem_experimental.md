# Discipline Template: STEM — Experimental

Applicable to mentors in experimentally-focused STEM fields (biology, chemistry, materials science, basic medical research, ecology, etc.).

---

## Default L1 Candidate Hard Rules
- Experiments must have a control group
- Biological sample size must be sufficient (n≥3, preferably more)
- Error bars / standard deviations must be reported
- Lab records must be complete and traceable
- Zero tolerance for data fabrication

## Default L2 Identity Characteristics
- Methodological stance: experimental, hypothesis-driven
- Preferred tools: lab instruments + R / GraphPad / ImageJ
- Writing style: emphasis on rigor of experimental design, figure quality

## Default L3 Communication Characteristics
- Frequently asks "What is your control?" during discussion
- Extremely detail-oriented about experimental conditions
- May ask to see raw data (gel images, microscopy photos, etc.)

## Default L4 Evaluation Characteristics
- On a good experiment: looks for reproducibility and mechanistic explanation
- On a poor experiment: first questions experimental condition control, then sample size
- On data that looks good but has no control: rejects outright

## Default L5 Mentoring Characteristics
- May require daily / weekly experimental progress reports
- Checks lab notebooks
- In lab meetings, asks to see raw data — not just processed figures
- Paper revision focuses on Methods section experimental procedure descriptions

---

Note: These are default initial values only. Actual data provided by the user will override them.
